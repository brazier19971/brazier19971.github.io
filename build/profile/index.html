A short personal profile about myself...

Maximising opportunities, being bold, always learning. These are the three main principles that define how I approach life. I’m never afraid to step up to a new challenge, and I prefer to approach large, complex situations by breaking them down into smaller sub-tasks. When things don’t go as expected, I take the time to think about what can be done to improve the situation, instead of shying away and waiting for someone else to sort it.

I strongly appreciate and value the importance of teamwork, and I firmly believe that many minds working together is greater than one. Forming constructive relationships between individuals is of key importance of any team that I work in, which helps to facilitate effective communication, and to strengthen the team as a whole. Enhancing my leadership skills is something I am keen to do. I was recognised with the ‘Governors' prize for leadership in the Sixth Form’ in 2019 as part of my Sixth Form enrichment programme.

Notably, at present, in addition to being a full-time student, I also work as a shift supervisor at a local convenience store. This was one of the boldest decisions I have taken in my career so far. Being one of the society’s youngest supervisors, starting the role at 20, it certainly was a move that carried risk. However, I have thoroughly enjoyed the additional responsibility. Leading the team effectively is of crucial importance to the store’s operation.

One particular interest I have stems from the earliest years of my childhood, and that is broadcast technology. In around 2004, our family had our first digital satellite video recorder installed, and I remember watching the engineer pausing the TV show right in front of me. Four-year-old me was completely mesmerised by this ‘magic’ as I used to call it. I spent many hours staring at the demonstration channel trying to figure out how it all worked. Nowadays, I maintain this interest, with the key advantage of knowing that it isn’t ‘magic’. Recently, I have begun to learn the fundamentals of DVB-S and its underlying principles, and one of my spare-time projects involves utilising the serial ports on a range of satellite receivers to simulate previously discontinued functionality related to an audio service integrated into its electronic programming guide (EPG).
